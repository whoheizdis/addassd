Letting go made room for living.


Finally Living, Not Just Existing

By Helena Marie B. Samson || Published: 05/02/2026 at 11:11 am


There was a point when my days started to blur together. I was present, but not really alive. I followed routines, met expectations, and did what was required of me—but nothing felt intentional. I was surviving, not living.

Something shifted when I realized how much of myself I was holding back. I was waiting for the “right time” to be brave, happy, or fulfilled. But that time never arrived. Life kept moving, and I was standing still, watching it pass.

Choosing to live meant making uncomfortable decisions. It meant saying no without guilt, chasing things that scared me, and accepting that not everyone would understand my choices. Living required courage, not perfection.

Now, I’m learning to take up space in my own life. To feel deeply, to try honestly, and to believe that I deserve more than just getting by. I don’t have everything figured out—but for the first time, I’m truly living.
