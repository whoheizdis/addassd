Being enough doesn’t mean being wanted.


A Heart That Learned Love the Hard Way

By Helena Marie B. Samson || Published: 02/02/2026 at 06:07 am


I didn’t learn love through gentle beginnings or fairytale endings. I learned it through silence, miscommunication, and moments where loving someone felt heavier than letting go. I gave too much of myself without knowing how to ask for the same in return. I believed that love meant enduring, even when it hurt.

There were times I mistook effort for obligation and pain for passion. I stayed in situations where I kept explaining my worth instead of being valued naturally. I thought that if I loved hard enough, things would eventually feel right. Instead, I learned that love cannot survive on hope alone.

Heartbreak has a way of changing you quietly. It doesn’t just end a relationship—it reshapes the way you trust, the way you open up, and the way you protect your heart. I became more cautious, more observant, and less willing to ignore red flags just to avoid being alone.

Even so, I don’t regret loving the way I did. That version of me was honest and brave. My heart learned love the hard way, but it also learned resilience. And one day, it will recognize a love that feels safe instead of painful.
