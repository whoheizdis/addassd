Not all pain is punishment—some is direction.


When Both Choices Still Hurt

By Helena Marie B. Samson || Published: 03/02/2026 at 10:11 pm


Some choices don’t come with relief. No matter which direction you take, there’s something you have to leave behind. I’ve been in moments where every option carried its own kind of pain—stay and feel stuck, or leave and feel lost. There was no clear “right” answer, only consequences.

Making those decisions felt isolating. People often assume there’s always a better choice, but they don’t see the emotional weight behind it. They don’t feel the guilt, the doubt, or the fear of regretting what you didn’t choose. Standing in the middle of those choices felt like being pulled apart.

What I’ve learned is that pain doesn’t always mean failure. Sometimes it’s just part of choosing growth. Walking away can hurt just as much as holding on, but one pain leads to healing while the other keeps you in the same place. Not every choice is about comfort—some are about becoming who you need to be.

In the end, choosing yourself can feel selfish, scary, and lonely. But it can also be the first step toward peace. Even when both choices hurt, choosing forward still matters.
